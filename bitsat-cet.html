BITSAT CET
Exam Overview Of BITSAT
What is BITSAT?
  • The Birla Institute of Technology & Science conducts the BIT Science Aptitude Test (BITSAT) every year.
  • There are three institutions that consider BITSAT scores.
  • This university-level examination is conducted in order to provide admission to UG and PG courses.
Exam Pattern Of BITSAT
  • Subjects: Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics or Biology
  • Type of questions: Objective (MCQs)
  • Mode of examination: Online
  • Exam duration: 3 hours
  • Number of questions: 150 (450 marks)
  • Marking scheme: 3 marks awarded for each correct answer. Negative marking 1 mark deducted for each incorrect response
  • Note: If you finish the 150 questions in less than the allotted time, there are 12 bonus questions too. Remember that you have to necessarily attempt all 150 questions and can’t go back to them once you’ve taken up the bonus section.
  • Physics: 40 Questions
  • Chemistry: 40 Questions
  • Mathematics/Biology: 45 Questions
  • English (Proficiency) and Logical Reasoning: 15+10 (25) Questions
  • Total: 150 Questions
Eligibility Criteria For BITSAT
  • Subjects: You must have compulsorily studied Physics, Chemistry and Maths/Biology in your 10+2 or equivalent examination.
  • 12th standard or equivalent examination: You must have passed the 12th examination of a 10+2 system from a recognized Central or State Board or its equivalent examination.
  • Score: You must have passed your 10+2 with an aggregate of 75% in Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics or Biology.
  • You must have secured 60% in each of the three subjects.
  • You must be proficient in English.
  • Age: Only those candidates who are appearing in Class 12 in the same year as the year of examination or have cleared Class 12 one year prior to giving BITSAT, are eligible.
  • Top rank holders: If you are a top ranker or a candidate with Rank 1 in Central or State Boards in India, you will be offered admission to the programme of your choice; irrespective of your score or rank in BITSAT (as long as you meet the above criteria).
Registration For BITSAT
  • Step 1: Filling the application form
  • Visit the BITSAT official website and click on ‘Apply here’ for BITSAT
    Enter your basic details like name, gender, date of birth, email ID and nationality
    Enter your examination details like score, name of institute, year of passing etc.
  • Step 2: Choose your test centre
  • Choose 3 test centres, as per your preference (unless you choose Dubai, then you don’t need to select any other centres)
    Verify all the details and confirm your submission
    Note down your ‘Unique Registration Number’
  • Step 3: Payment of fees
  • You can make a payment offline via ICICI Bank Challan payment or through the online mode using a Credit Card, Debit Card or Net Banking
    Don’t forget to take a printout of your filled out application form for future reference
  • Step 4: Final Step
  • Procedure post submission of application form Keep checking the BITSAT application form status to ensure that your application request has been acknowledged.
    If you discover any mistake in the application form that you’ve submitted, you can edit or correct the form details till the first week of March usually. However, it is recommended to keep a tab on the actual dates on the official website of BITSAT.
Application Fee For BITSAT
The application fee is subject to vary from year to year. Please check the official website of that year for information related to Application Fee
Admit Card For BITSAT
  • The admit card is available once you have booked your slots for the entrance exam as per the schedule and available slots in any exam centre
  • The admit cards will be available from 2nd week of April [Please check the exact date on the official website of BITSAT]
  • You should preserve the admit card till the end of the admission process
  • Without the admit card, no candidate will be allowed to enter the exam centre

All You Need To Know About BITS Pilani

The Birla Institute of Technology & Science, BITS Pilani is an all-India Institute for higher education. The Institute is a dream come true of its founder late Mr. G.D.Birla – an eminent industrialist, a participant in the Indian freedom struggle and a close associate of the Father of Indian Nation late Mr. Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (Mahatma Gandhi). What started in the early 1900s as a small school, blossomed into a set of colleges for higher education, ranging from the Humanities to Engineering until 1964 when all these colleges amalgamated to culminate into a unique Indian University of International standing. This university was christened as the Birla Institute of Technology and Science, Pilani, known to many as BITS, Pilani. Over the years, BITS has provided the highest quality technical education to students from all over India admitted on the basis of merit. Its graduates may be found throughout the world in all areas of engineering, science, and commerce. BITS symbolizes the maturing of the Indian technical ability and “can-do” entrepreneurial spirit, especially as derived from the private sector. BITS is located in the Vidya Vihar campus adjacent to the town of Pilani in Rajasthan. Pilani is the hometown of the Birla family and has a population of about 50,000. It is about 200 km west of Delhi and about 220 km north of Jaipur. The Institute buildings, hostels and residential quarters for staff with neatly laid out roads, lawns and gardens constitute the BITS Campus of about 328 acres.

All You Need To Know About BITS Hyderabad

BITS Hyderabad has one of the most beautiful college campuses in India. It is planned for this to become the largest BITS campus. One drawback, however, is the distance from the main city of Hyderabad-27 km. BITS Pilani, Hyderabad campus has been equipped with the latest technology, which reflects in the labs, classrooms, and hostels. All hostels are single occupancy, right from the first year onwards. They are equipped with other basic facilities, such as 24×7 WiFi, TVs, TT tables, chess boards, etc. There are 6 hostels for boys and 2 for girls. The campus also has other basic facilities like sports grounds (football, tennis, etc), a bank, clinic, computer centre, etc.

Academics: The academic course structure of BITS Goa is governed by the university of BITS Pilani. However, each campus has its own autonomous administration, student welfare division, faculty recruitment and placement division. Being an autonomous institute, students also have options of other programs apart from the ones they are admitted in. This may be done by aid of various systems.

Dual Degree System: BITS Pilani, Goa Campus offers a dual degree to the students of Master of Science programs. Under the dual degree scheme, a student is offered two separate degrees on graduation : a degree in Master of Science (Hons.) and in Bachelor of Engineering (Hons.) However, the program increases the duration of his/her stay on campus by at least two semesters. The compulsory disciplinary courses of the second program are completed in the seventh and eighth semesters. The final year (ninth and tenth semester) is dedicated for two, semester long, off campus industrial training internships or master’s thesis.

Program Transfer: BITS Goa also offers flexibility in opting for other courses or to transfer from one program to another at the end of first year, if the student has performed well in academics.

If the student is in the top 10 among full batch of students of the campus in terms of CGPA after their first year, then it is allowed for the student to take a B.E. + B.E. dual degree.

If the student is in the top 30 students of the campus in terms of CGPA after their first year, one can abandon their degree for another, i.e. transfer from one program to another. BITS Goa also gives due importance to Humanities and Arts and it is mandatory for a student to take at least three humanity courses in total.

All You Need To Know About BITS GOA

BITS Goa, which was founded in 2004, is a technical and science institute for higher education in India. The craze of experiencing college life in a place like Goa along with the great quality of curriculum and teaching methodology of BITS Goa is what makes a student desire a seat in this institution. The campus of BITS Goa is located in Zuarinagar, Goa, on the banks of the Zuari River. Around 2600 students reside in the campus. The campus of BITS Goa was listed as number 7 in the list of the most beautiful campuses in India by HolidayIQ.com. Situated in the surroundings of the bank of river Zuari, the campus boasts of a unique scenic beauty and panoramic view of waterways, forests, landscapes and hillocks. The BITS Pilani K K Birla Goa Campus, is about 10 kms south of Vasco-da-Gama, 20 kms north of Margaon and 7 kms east of Goa Airport, along National Highway – 17B, Bypass road. The Campus houses the Main building, having academic and administrative offices, Guest House, Hostels for boys and girls, Student Activity Centre, faculty and staff quarters, Medical Centre, Playground and Shopping Complex. As far as sports facilities is concerned, BITS Goa encourages its students for active participation in different sports and for the same along with a common playground and courts for Basketball, Tennis and Badminton, BITS Goa Campus has a BCCI-recognized cricket ground maintained by the Goa Cricket Association that has hosted several national level tournaments such as the Vijay Hazare Trophy and a football ground maintained by Salgaocar F.C.

Exam Overview
What is MHT-CET?

MHT - CET is conducted by the Department of Technical Education, Maharashtra It facilitates admission to full time degree courses of health sciences and technical education in colleges of Maharashtra. It includes courses like: MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BHMS, Engineering and Technology, Pharmacy and Pharm. D.
Exam Pattern Of MH-CET
MHT-CET consists of two papers:
Paper 1: Physics
Chemistry Paper 2: Mathematics
Paper 3: Biology (Botany and Zoology)
  • Paper 1
    • Subjects: Physics and Chemistry
    • Duration: 90 minutes
    • Questions: 100 (50 each)
    • Marking scheme: 1 mark for every correct answer
    • Negative marking: No negative marking
  • Paper 2
    • Subjects: Mathematics
    • Duration: 90 minutes
    • Questions: 50
    • Marking scheme: 2 mark for every correct answer
    • Negative marking: No negative marking
  • Paper 3
    • Subjects: Biology (Botany and Zoology)
    • Duration: 90 minutes
    • Questions: 100 (50 Each)
    • Marking scheme: 1 mark for every correct answer
    • Negative marking: No negative marking
Eligibility Criteria For MH-CET
  • Age: Minimum age requirement is 17 years on or before 31st December of the year of admission.
  • The age limit is 25 years for a general category candidate.
  • The age limit for SC and ST category student is 30 years.
  • Subjects: Compulsory subjects include Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics/Biology.
  • Score in 12th standard or equivalent examination: As a general category candidate, you must have scored a minimum of 50% in your 10+2 or equivalent examination.
  • As an SC, ST or OBC candidate, you must have scored a minimum of 45%.
  • Education: You must have passed or must be appearing for your 12th standard or equivalent examination.
  • Number of attempts: You can only attempt MHT-CET 3 times.
  • Domicile You must have a domicile certificate of Maharashtra.
